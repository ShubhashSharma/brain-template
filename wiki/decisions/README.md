# Decisions

Every decision that is hard to reverse. One page per decision.

---

## Why this folder might be the most valuable folder you have
At 8 figures, a wrong supplier call can cost six figures in landed bad inventory. A wrong price test can flatten a launch. A wrong brand pivot can kill a category.

A decision log is cheap insurance. Three months from now, when something is broken, the question is always: "what did we change, and why?" Without a log, you guess. With one, you read.

---

## Naming
`YYYY-MM-short-title.md`. Examples:
- `2026-02-acme-shampoo-price-test.md`
- `2025-11-shenzhen-supplier-switch.md`
- `2026-04-bfcm-discount-strategy.md`

---

## When to log
Anything that is one-way (or hard) to reverse:
- Price changes
- Supplier swaps
- New SKU launches (gate decision)
- Discontinuing a SKU
- Brand positioning changes
- Major agency or hire decisions
- Claims / packaging changes

Trivial stuff (PPC bid tweaks, daily ops) do not need a decision page.

---

## Always include reversibility + a follow-up date
If you can't say "by DATE we will know if this worked, by checking METRIC", you haven't decided yet, you've just expressed an opinion.
