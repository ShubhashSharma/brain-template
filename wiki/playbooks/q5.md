# Q5 - the post-Christmas window

The three weeks from 26 December through ~15 January. Returns spike, gift cards drive a final shopping wave, ad CPMs collapse, and the smartest operators bank Q5 sales while everyone else takes their foot off.

---

## When to run
- Trigger: 20 December every year.
- Lead time required: most prep is in BFCM. Q5 itself is execution + returns management.

---

## What's actually happening in Q5

| Wave | Window | Driver |
|---|---|---|
| Returns wave | Dec 26 - Jan 10 | Gift-recipients returning unwanted items |
| Gift card wave | Dec 26 - Jan 7 | $30B+ in unspent gift cards activating |
| New-year-new-me | Jan 1 - Jan 21 | Wellness, fitness, planners, beauty, organisation |
| CPM collapse | Dec 26 - Jan 5 | Big-budget brands paused, paid traffic gets cheap |

---

## Pre-Q5 checklist

### T-2 weeks (mid December)
- [ ] Confirm returns policy is live and visible on every listing.
- [ ] Brief CS team on expected ticket volume.
- [ ] Stock buffer for gift card / new-year wave: keep 3-4 weeks of cover.
- [ ] Plan ad spend envelope for Dec 26 - Jan 7 (low CPM window).

### T-1 week (week of 19 Dec)
- [ ] Refunds reserve set aside (~10% of BFCM revenue is a starting estimate).
- [ ] Returns processing capacity confirmed at 3PL.
- [ ] Email/SMS flow scheduled for Dec 26 (first wave) and Jan 1 (new-year wave).

---

## During Q5

### Day-of (each of the 21 days)
- [ ] 06:00 local: yesterday's sales + returns count. Flag any SKU with returns >15%.
- [ ] Mid-day: ad spend pacing in the cheap-CPM window. Push budget to capture.
- [ ] End of day: snapshot. Update returns running total.

---

## Returns management (the underrated piece)

The dollar value of returns is one number. The operational cost is bigger:
- Reimbursement gap: returned items reimbursed at less than cost
- Long-term storage fees on returned-and-not-resaleable inventory
- Brand impact: a 1-star "didn't fit" review three weeks after Christmas

Specific actions:
- [ ] Run reimbursement reconciliation by Jan 10. Flag missing reimbursements.
- [ ] Triage returned units: resaleable, FBA-graded, FBA-unsellable, write-off.
- [ ] Assess top return reasons. Anything above 5% of returns is a listing or product issue.

---

## What NOT to do
- Don't kill ad spend Dec 26-31. CPMs are at their lowest of the year.
- Don't ignore returns to focus on new sales. The reimbursement gap quietly eats CM3 if you don't audit.
- Don't launch new SKUs on Jan 1. Wait for the new-year wave to reveal itself before adding noise.

---

## Post-Q5 retro

By 20 January, capture:
- Sales by week (Dec 23-29, Dec 30-Jan 5, Jan 6-12, Jan 13-19)
- Returns rate by SKU vs trailing 90d
- Reimbursement gap (reimbursed vs cost basis)
- Top 3 return reasons by SKU
- Ad spend efficiency in the low-CPM window

Update this playbook in place.
